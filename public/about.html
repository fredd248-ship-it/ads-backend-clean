About ADS

What is ADS?
Adaptive Decision System (ADS) is a structured feedback system that helps you understand your decisions over time. Instead of focusing on intentions, ADS tracks what you actually do and how those decisions turn out.
How ADS Evaluates Decisions
ADS does not rely on a single metric.

It evaluates decisions using multiple signals—how often you use a decision, how it performs over time, the conditions under which it was made (time pressure and emotional weight), and whether you would make the same decision again.

By combining these, ADS reveals patterns in your behavior that are difficult to recognize in isolation.
Why It Exists
Many decisions feel right in the moment but are only understood in hindsight. ADS exists to close that gap by showing how your decisions actually perform over time.
How It Works
You record a decision, live with the outcome, and evaluate it later.

ADS compares how often you use that decision with how well it performs, identifying patterns such as impulsive behavior, inconsistency, and overlooked successes.

These patterns are surfaced in the Insights section to help you better understand your decision-making over time.
What Makes It Different
ADS does not predict your future or tell you what to do. It reflects your actual behavior using structured data.

Every insight is based on your own decisions—not assumptions, opinions, or generic advice.
What This Is Not
ADS is not financial, medical, or professional advice. It is not a replacement for judgment.

It is a feedback system designed to help you recognize patterns and improve decision quality over time.
